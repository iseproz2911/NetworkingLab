VLSM (Variable Length Subnet Mask)

Subnets: S <= 2^n
Hosts/subnet: h <= 2^(32 - u - n) - 2
n: borrowing bits

* Find n1 to satisfy LAN 1 (60 hosts)
==> satisfy LAN 2, 3, 4 too

//major network: 192.168.1.0/24

60 <= 2^(32 - 24 - n1) - 2

==> 2^(8 - n1) >= 62

Let 2^(8 - n1) = 64 IPs = 2^6 > 62
==> n1 = 2 ==> /24 (+2) --> /26
& S1 = 2^n1 = 4 subnets

Sub0: 192.168.1.0 -- 64 IPs --> 192.168.1.63/26
//SM (26 bit 1): 255.255.255.192
//assign to LAN 1
==> Hosts: 192.168.1.1 --> 192.168.1.62/26

Sub1: 192.168.1.64 -- 64 IPs --> 192.168.1.127/26
Sub2: 192.168.1.128 -- 64 IPs --> 192.168.1.191/26	(2)
Sub3: 192.168.1.192 -- 64 IPs --> 192.168.1.255/26	(3)

* Find n2 to satisfy LAN 3 (30 hosts) from (2)
//start IP: 192.168.1.128 & u1 = 26

30 <= 2^(32 - 26 - n2) - 2
==> 2^(6 - n2) >= 32 = 2^5 IPs
==> n2 = 1 ==> /26 (+1) --> /27
& S2 = 2^n2 = 2

Sub20: 192.168.1.128 -- 32 IPs --> 192.168.1.159/27
//assign to LAN 3; SM: 255.255.255.224
Sub21: 192.168.1.160 -- 32 IPs --> 192.168.1.191/27
//assign to LAN 4

* Find n3 to satisfy R1 - R2; R2 - R3; R3 - R4; R4 - R1
from (3) 
//start IP: 192.168.1.192 & u1 = 26

2 <= 2^(32 - 26 - n3) - 2
==> 2^(6 - n3) >= 4 = 2^2 IPs
==> n3 = 4 ==> /26 (+4) --> /30
& S3 = 2^n3 = 16

R1-R2: 192.168.1.192 -- 4 IPs --> 192.168.1.195/30
//SM: 255.255.255.252
R2-R3: 192.168.1.196 -- 4 IPs --> 192.168.1.199/30
R3-R4: 192.168.1.200 -- 4 IPs --> 192.168.1.203/30
R4-R1: 192.168.1.204 -- 4 IPs --> 192.168.1.207/30

/24 --> /26 --> /27 --> /30
